Section 01

Thematic Core

Blood Court operates on three inseparable levels. The power of the story is that they cannot be untangled from one another.

I
Class Warfare

The violence of legacy. What wealthy institutions do to talented poor children who dare to be better. The scholarship program as a trap. The tennis court as a rigged game.

II
Inherited Evil

The horror of discovering that the people who raised you built the world around you to consume you. That love and instrumentalization can coexist. That a mother can genuinely love her daughter and still intend to use her as a vessel.

III
The Refusal

Whether blood destiny can be refused. What it costs to break a cycle. Whether breaking it is even possible — or whether refusal is just the cycle's next phase.

The jade necklace, the Salazar bloodline, and the cult of Huitzilopochtli form the mythological spine. The tennis club, the scholarship program, and the class divide form the social spine. Neither works without the other. The supernatural horror is the class horror made literal.

Section 02

The World

A small American town, late 2024 crossing into 2025. The year matters: 2025 marks the 50-year cycle of the Sneer Racquet Club's founding. The clock has run out. Everything in this film is happening because time is up.

Eastside Heights
The Hunger

Working-class neighborhood. Modest homes. Public transit. Recreation centers with chain-link courts and cracked concrete. Children practice against painted walls with rackets worn down to the wood. The talent here is real and has nowhere adequate to go.

Riverside District
The Comfort

Sprawling estates. Luxury cars. Private courts. Wealthy children take lessons from coaches. Their technique is competent but without the hunger of the children against the wall. They have been given everything except the need to be great.

The Sneer Racquet Club — The Bridge That Keeps Them Apart
The Machine

The club presents itself as a bridge between worlds. It is the opposite. Its architecture enforces separation: center courts (Legacy Members Only), middle courts (Platinum and Gold), outer courts (Scholarship Program and General Use). The sight lines are deliberately designed so legacy members can observe scholarship players, but scholarship players cannot see what happens on the center courts. This is not an accident. It is theology made physical.

The scholarship program identifies talented working-class children, brings them into proximity with legacy members, and when they inevitably defeat those legacy members' children — as talented children will — they are marked for sacrifice. The program is a feeding mechanism for Huitzilopochtli disguised as philanthropy.

Visual Key The club's logo is a single S. Every initiated cult member knows S stands for Salazar. Every uninitiated member believes it stands for Sneer. This deliberate ambiguity has protected the cult's secret for a century. Kracell's journey is partly about learning what the S really means.

Underground Geography

The dungeon — Huitzilopochtli's chamber, the ritual sacrifice room, the cellar of bodies — is directly beneath the tennis courts. This is not incidental. It is the film's title made literal.

Ceremonial Entrance
Club Office Stairs — Internal

Used by cult members. The ceremonial entrance. Connects the club's legitimate surface world directly to its underground horror. Kracell ascends these stairs in the film's climax and emerges into the office lobby — at which point she looks through a structural gap in the ceiling and sees the underside of Court 6's blacktop. She has been standing beneath the courts.

Operational Backdoor
Cliffside Forest Door — External

An iron door set into a cliff face in the forest, connected to a natural cave network that predates the club by centuries. The Salazar family discovered and consecrated it in 1925. Used to move victims and bodies without being seen from the club. The plaque: ESTABLISHED 1925. ORDER MUST BE MAINTAINED. Below, in genuinely older stone: S A L A Z A R.

Section 03

The Cult & Its God

The Salazar family were Spanish colonial nobility who encountered Aztec religion not as something to be destroyed but as something to be weaponized.

Origins

Over generations the Salazar family built their entire identity around ritual sacrifice. Those who dared exceed their social station were marked. This practice was carried in their blood and passed through their women. When the Salazars came to America in the early twentieth century, they anglicized their name to SNEER and founded the Sneer Tennis Club in 1925.

The Salazar name persists in three buried locations: the dungeon door plaque (barely legible in old stone), the office floor (carved into original stone beneath the boards), and the estate plaque (ghosted beneath the Sneer lettering, painted over but not quite gone). Planted early. Paid off in the climax.

Huitzilopochtli — The Beast

Locked Creative Decision Huitzilopochtli is never directly shown. Its presence is communicated through: the wrong-colored, moving light source from below; sub-bass sound design and Nahuatl frequency; and Tee's altered state when he comes back through the door. The creature the audience builds is always worse than anything practical.

Huitzilopochtli is not a metaphor. It is not symbolic. It is a genuine living entity — ancient, consuming, and real. It lives beneath the tennis courts. The Salazars did not create it. They found it, consecrated it, and built their institution around its hunger.

Communication

Speaks in Nahuatl — its native tongue. Rarely. When it does, the words carry the weight of something that has always won.

Vulnerability

Cannot be killed by conventional means. Grows weaker without feeding — not more violent, but uncontrolled. Fire is its most significant threat. Whether fire can truly kill it remains unresolved in Film 1. This ambiguity is intentional.

The Feeding Mechanism

Fed continuously since 1925 — scholarship children, one at a time. The cult selects victims by a theological criterion: a poor child defeating a wealthy child is a cosmic violation of order. The sacrifice restores balance. Seven children in the current cycle. Bobby is the eighth.

Blood Recognition

Recognizes Salazar blood. The jade bracelets are its earliest signal — passive, always-running attunement objects worn from childhood. The necklace is its full activation. The beast knows Kracell from the New Year's Eve ceremony. Her blood is in it.

The 50-Year Cycle

In addition to the continuous feeding of scholarship sacrifices, the cult's mythology requires a macro-event every fifty years: the queen sacrifice and succession. A scholarship child is a meal. The queen is a feast.

1925
Club Founded — Eleanor Becomes First Queen
Eleanor Salazar founds the Sneer Tennis Club and becomes the first American queen. The 50-year cycle begins.
1950
Eleanor Removes the Necklace
Eleanor deliberately removes the necklace when Dotty is born so she will age faster — to convincingly perform the role of grandmother by 1975 when the succession ceremony comes.
1975
First American Succession — Eleanor Sacrificed, Dotty Becomes Queen
Dotty kills Eleanor and participates in the ritual consumption as the definitive act of devotion. The necklace is placed on Dotty, age 25. Her aging halves. Jack receives the jade bracelet as co-leader.
2024
New Year's Eve Blood Binding Ceremony
Kracell's blood is introduced to the beast so it can recognize her as the incoming queen. The preparatory ritual. Not the succession — that comes next year.
2025
Scheduled Succession — Kracell Refuses
Dotty accepts her death. The beast is fed by Dotty's immolation. But Kracell refuses to put the necklace on. The transfer does not complete. The beast is fed but has no queen. The cycle is disrupted — not broken.

The Jade Objects

The Necklace
Dotty's — Always

The primary Salazar power object. Slows the reigning queen's aging to half speed as long as worn. Never leaves Dotty's throat until Kracell breaks it off in the dungeon climax and sets it at Dotty's feet. Kracell does not pick it up. That is the refusal.

Kracell's Bracelet
Attunement — Always Running

Worn since childhood. Passive attunement object — not power but orientation. Explains her investigative obsession, her certainty about Doe, the cold precision in her violence. She also wears Bobby's bracelet on the same wrist. Both in the final image. Neither ever removed.

Mary's Bracelet
The Contingency

Newer, more deliberate in construction. Dotty placed it on her recently, knowing the timeline was approaching. Earlier in the attunement process. Less shaped. More shapeable.

Jack's Bracelet
Co-Leader Rank

Received at the 1975 ceremony when Jack was installed as co-leader. Slows his aging at the same rate as Dotty's necklace — reward from Huitzilopochtli for service and complicity. Both appear the same apparent age despite being ~75 in 2025.

Sue
The Cover — Dotty's Biological Sister

Eleanor's older daughter. Passed over for the queen succession. Ages normally — no jade artifact, no necklace. Performs as Dotty's mother from 1975 onward because the visual gap between them grows convincing over time: by 2025 she appears ~65–68 while Dotty appears ~50. The cover story doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be what untouchable people tell the club. Survives the fire. The machine did not die in that fire.

Ted
The Loyalty — Sue's Husband

No Salazar blood. No jade artifact. Married into the cult through Sue. Performs as Dotty's father. The cult sweetens the arrangement: Ted and Sue hold the highest ceremonial positions below Dotty — red robes at every ceremony, inner circle authority. His devotion is not blood-deep. It is chosen, total, and fifty years old. Survives the fire alongside Sue. Operational.

Section 04

The New Year's Eve Ceremony

Six months before the film's present-day events. The blood binding ceremony. Not the queen succession — the preparatory ritual. Kracell's blood is introduced to the beast so it can recognize her as the incoming queen.

Twenty-eight robed, masked congregants. Ted and Sue in red robes at the head — the highest ceremonial positions below Dotty. Dotty at the still center. A ceremonial blade. Small precise cut. Kracell's blood fills a bowl. She trusts her mother. That is the point.

Dotty descends the stairs into absolute darkness. The congregation waits in silence. From below: a resonance felt in the sternum, not heard with the ears. The beast acknowledging the blood. Recognizing the incoming queen for the first time. Dotty returns. The bowl is empty.

Three-Pass Structure

The ceremony is revealed across the film in three passes — each pass deepening the audience's understanding of what happened that night.

Pass One
Beat 5, Act One
The Confused Present-Tense Experience
The ceremony as Kracell's confused present-tense experience — from outside her own body. She trusts Dotty. In the supply closet: Tee, pressed against the back wall. Mary, 8, beside him. She has been there the whole time. She watches with complete absorption and no fear.
Pass Two
Vision, Beat 14
The Queen's Blood Shared
Triggered by Kracell touching her jade bracelet at the camp. The bracelet is hot. Dotty's blood, her own wrists cut, poured into the punch bowl. The congregation drinking from it. The queen's blood shared with her followers. Chaotic close-ups only. 30 seconds of screen time. The bracelet pulls Kracell straight into Pass Three without releasing her.
Pass Three
Vision, Beat 15
The Blood Delivered to the Beast
Dotty at the bottom of the stone stairs. The bowl set down. The presence in the dark — the resonance moving through the stone floor. The bowl's contents drawn upward and gone. The beast's eyes. One line in Nahuatl: "The blood remembers what the mind forgets." The bowl empties. The eyes go last.
Architectural Note — Protected The three-vision sequence runs back to back with no bathroom return between them. The bracelet holds Kracell through all three. One bathroom return only — at the end of Pass Three. This architecture is correct and must be protected.
Section 05

Characters

K
Kracell Sneer
18  ·  True Salazar Heir  ·  Primary
Salazar Blood Jade Bracelet Bobby's Bracelet

Kracell has been the designated queen-in-waiting her entire life without knowing it. She is methodical, observant, and carries a certainty that the world is more wrong than people are admitting. Her investigation into the missing scholarship children precedes the film — she has been tracking this for months before Bobby is taken.

The jade bracelet has been attuning her to the cult's frequency since childhood. This explains her obsessive investigative drive, her certainty about Doe when everyone else is skeptical, the cold precision in her violence. She is not evil. She is oriented toward evil — and fighting it, using tools the evil gave her.

Kracell begins the film as an investigator trying to understand a mystery from the outside. She ends it having discovered she was never on the outside. The mystery was built around her. The refusal — refusing to put the necklace on after Dotty's death — is the first fully self-determined act of her life.

"I expected much more from a Salazar." — Kracell to Dotty, final confrontation
T
Tee (Cuahtemoc)
20  ·  Jack's Son  ·  Primary
No Salazar Blood Carries a Secret

Tee is Jack's biological son from a relationship with Maria Lopez. He carries no Salazar blood. He was brought into the Sneer household as an infant after Jack and Dotty's confrontation over his existence. Dotty tried to kill him as a baby. Jack stopped her. They raised him as their heir — which means Dotty raised him as a performance of heir while engineering Kracell's succession behind everyone's back.

Tee witnessed the blood binding ceremony from outside the threshold. He drove himself there because something felt wrong about where Dotty was taking Kracell that night. He saw the congregation, saw Kracell in the center, saw the bowl, heard the resonance from below. He has told no one. This is the specific fear underneath his protectiveness throughout the film.

His arc is about choosing his own identity against the identity his parents tried to assign him. His line in the dungeon — "I don't care what you chose for me. I'm my own person. I don't want this and I never will" — is the first time in his life he has said that sentence out loud and meant it.

D
Dorothy "Dotty" Sneer
Apparent ~50 / Actual 75  ·  Reigning Queen
Reigning Queen Salazar Blood The Necklace

Dotty is Eleanor Salazar's actual biological daughter — not granddaughter. Born 1950. She became queen in 1975, age 25, by killing her own mother Eleanor and participating in the ritual consumption as her definitive act of devotion. She has been approximately fifty years old in appearance since that ceremony.

The jade necklace has been on her throat every day for fifty years. It is automatic — the way you stop noticing a watch. When she clasps it at her vanity each night, something shifts in her bearing she never notices. She has never existed without it as an adult.

Dotty loves Kracell because Kracell is the Salazar succession — the fulfillment of fifty years of work. The love and the project are completely inseparable. Both are entirely genuine. She also tried to kill Tee as a baby because he represented a deviation from the project. That was the cult's theology applied without sentiment. Saul must write both truths simultaneously.

Dotty is willing to die. More than willing — she has been preparing for this death for decades. When the kerosene hits, she goes quiet. She understands: this is the ceremony, imperfect but sufficient. She watches Jack burn without flinching. Kracell's apparent act of punishment is an unwitting completion of the ritual. That is the horror underneath the apparent victory.

"She has ten years before she needs to understand anything. You'd have been done by now." — Dotty on Mary
J
Jack Wellington
Apparent ~50 / Actual 75  ·  Cult Co-Leader
Co-Leader Jade Bracelet Oxford — Mesoamerican Ritual

Jack is the most beautiful man in any room he enters. Black Caribbean, Oxford-educated, broad-shouldered in pristine whites. He studied ancient religions, comparative mythology, and pre-Columbian ritual practice — graduating with a thesis on sacrificial cosmology in Mesoamerican sun cults. He is as fluent in the theological architecture of Huitzilopochtli as he is in the mechanics of a perfect serve.

When Dotty found him at Oxford in 1968, she found a man who had spent years studying the intellectual architecture of exactly what she was building. His complicity in the cult is therefore not reluctant or gradual. He leaned toward it. He knew what it was. He chose it with full awareness because it was the most interesting thing he had ever been offered.

Jack does not know the succession ceremony involves Dotty's death. He thinks the ceremony is about transferring power and leadership — not about his wife being consumed. He is a monster who has been used by a larger monster without knowing it. His death by fire is the one moment in the film where the audience might feel a complicated flicker — not sympathy, but a recognition that he was also, in his way, instrumentalized.

"As long as you two live, this legacy is yours." — Jack, in Doe's memory
◊
John Doe (Necalli)
31  ·  Framed / Forest Operative
Cannot Speak 95% Hearing Loss Notepad Communication

True surname: Benally. Indigenous American. Framed by the cult for the disappearances he was actually trying to prevent. He has been living in the forest outside the club for months, surveilling its operations, building an evidence wall that makes Kracell's evidence board look preliminary by comparison.

Kracell trusts him before she has a logical reason to — the bracelet recognizes something in him. Their fist-bump shorthand is the film's warmest recurring gesture. He is the alliance the story needs.

Doe vanishes before dawn in the final sequence — no goodbye scene. He goes somewhere outside Kearney's jurisdiction. He carries Bobby's documentation. The hope is real. The path is difficult and uncertain. Write his departure as purposeful, not hopeful.

B
Bobby
16  ·  Catalyst  ·  The Standard
Eighth Victim

Bobby is what a person who genuinely sees Kracell looks like — warm, specific, paying attention to her in ways her family never has. His murder is not just her motivation. It is her proof that the world is exactly as wrong as she suspected.

Bobby was investigating before he was taken. The box in his closet contains tournament records, names of legacy members whose children lost to scholarship kids, documentation naming Sheriff Kearney as a cult member by name, and — in his third folder — the name Marcus Webb, 16 (club semifinals three weeks ago, defeated a legacy member's child). Bobby found Marcus Webb before Dotty did. The box is his last act of love.

"You're never invisible to me, Kracell. Not ever." — Bobby's last phone call
M
Mary
10 (turning 11)  ·  The Contingency
Salazar Blood Jade Bracelet Backup Queen

Mary is Dotty's contingency — a Salazar bloodline girl quietly cultivated as the backup queen in case Kracell refuses. Jack and Dotty's biological younger daughter. Kracell's biological sister. Dotty placed the jade bracelet on her recently, knowing the timeline was approaching.

Mary was at the New Year's Eve ceremony — further back, understanding even less than Tee. She saw Kracell stand in the center of something ceremonial and important and understood: someday that will be me. Not with dread. With solemn, patient readiness. That patience is the most disturbing thing about her.

She appears in exactly three scenes: background at the party (bracelet visible, no one looks), a photograph in Bobby's third folder (Dotty's hand on her shoulder, bracelet catching light), and the cruiser's back seat (asleep, bracelet in morning light, patient expression that sleep does not erase). In each: no emphasis. Just there.

E
Eleanor Salazar
1906–1975  ·  The Original Queen
First American Queen Salazar Originator

The originating Salazar queen who brought the necklace and the cult from the family's Spanish colonial lineage to America. Founded the Sneer Tennis Club in 1925 at age 19. Married Harold Sneer (white Anglo, social cover) and anglicized Salazar to Sneer.

Wore the necklace 1925–1950, then deliberately removed it so she would age faster — to convincingly perform the role of grandmother by 1975 when the succession came. In 2025 she would have appeared approximately 57 when sacrificed at 69. The cover story held because Eleanor engineered it to hold.

She is Dotty's actual biological mother — not grandmother, as the club was told. She had two daughters: Sue (born ~1947, passed over for succession) and Dotty (born 1950, the chosen heir).

The 50-Year Aging Timeline

Both Dotty and Jack appear to be in their late 40s to early 50s in 2025. Both are actually approximately 75. The jade artifacts halve the rate of aging — they do not freeze it. Over fifty calendar years, each has aged only twenty-five years in appearance.

Sue ages normally. In 2025 she is approximately 78, appears ~65–68 due to proximity to cult rituals. The visual gap between her and Dotty — approximately 15–18 apparent years — reads convincingly as mother and daughter. Nobody at the club has ever had reason to look closely. The Sneers are royalty there.

Section 07

Story Structure

Three acts. Eight sequences. Target 120–128 pages. Everything in this film is happening because the 50-year clock ran out. The Joseph fake-out is removed from the cold open and restored to its correct chronological position in Act Three. The title card appears once — at the opening, naming a geography.

Kracell crouching to her level: "I expected much more from a Salazar." The pocket knife under the necklace chain. The chain breaks. Set at Dotty's feet. Precisely. Not thrown.
27
The Kerosene / Doe's Match
Doe with the ordinary matchbox. He turns it once. He looks at Jack. "I'm sorry about your sister." The longest silence in the film. He opens the matchbox. He lets the flame burn toward his fingers — he has been waiting three years and eight months. He drops the match. HARD CUT TO: the tunnel. Running — purposeful, not panicked. Dotty watching her husband burn with her eyes open. She does not flinch. She does not scream. The resonance from the deadbolted door below. Kracell feels it in her sternum. She keeps running.
"I'm sorry about your sister" is the match, not the flame. He knows Jack means it. He knows it changes nothing. He drops the match anyway.
28–30
The Drive / The Camp / The Final Images
The police cruiser. Joseph climbing in without being asked. Whitney Houston — "How Will I Know." Kracell turns it off. Then Tee's hand reaching forward more slowly. He turns it back on. She does not turn it off again. The camp: Kracell cleaning Tee's wound. "You and me." Tee nods toward Joseph: "And him." Doe folds the Ted and Sue note and places it on the milk crate beside the fire where Tee has been sitting. He goes. The loading bay door closes quietly. Mary against the wall, eyes open, watching Kracell sleep. Kracell's face holds an almost pleased expression. Her fingers drift upward — finding the hollow of her throat. The empty space where the necklace was. Tracing circles on bare skin. The smile widens. Fractionally. It is too similar to Dotty's smile. FADE TO BLACK.
Final image: Kracell's jade bracelet and Bobby's bracelet both on her wrist. No title card. The fade to black is the last thing.
Section 08

State of Play at Film 1's End

What is true when the screen goes black. The cycle is not broken. It is delayed.

Jack
Dead. Burned in the dungeon.
Dotty
Dead. Accepted the fire as sufficient sacrifice. The beast fed on her death regardless of whether the ceremony was formal.
Huitzilopochtli
Fed but unqueened. No one is holding the leash. An unqueened Huitzilopochtli is not dying — it is uncontrolled.
The Necklace
On the dungeon floor. Jade does not burn. It survived. Someone will find it. The film does not show who. Intentional.
Ted and Sue / Kearney
The cult is headless but not dead. Ted and Sue are operational. Kearney is alive — the cult's most powerful remaining institutional asset. Bobby's box names him. The danger did not die in the fire.
Kracell
Refused the necklace. Still wearing the bracelet. The bracelet is still running. She won the battle. The war is ongoing.
Mary
Alive. Patient. Has the bloodline. Has the bracelet. Has ten years before she comes of age for the ceremony. Watched Kracell refuse and felt no dread.
John Doe
Gone. Legal situation unresolved. Heading somewhere outside Kearney's jurisdiction. Carries Bobby's documentation. The hope is real.
Bri
Locked inside. Safe. The blade photographs on her phone. Unresolved and dangerous to the cult.
Joseph
Alive. Traumatized. Chosen family. "And him."
Kracell saved Mary from the cult. She did not save the world from Mary. The sequel's horror: the girl who refused the crown is now the guardian of the girl who wants it.
